1 1 "The Looney Beginning" Glen Kennedy, Dave Marshall, Ken Boyer, Rich Arons Paul Dini, Sherri Stoner Barry Caldwell, Bryon Vaughns September 14, 1990 KC/W-148
A fictionalized origin story for the series. Taking inspiration from Bugs Bunny, an animator attempts to create rabbit characters, Buster and Babs Bunny, but discards the sketches.
2 2 "A Quack in the Quarks" Art Vitello Tom Minton Douglas McCarthy, Bruce Timm September 17, 1990 A-140
A loose Star Wars parody. Plucky Duck has to save a planet called Planet X from "Duck Vader".
3 3 "The Wheel o' Comedy" Art Vitello Paul Dini, Tom Minton, Gordon Bressack, Charles Howell Bruce Timm, Douglas McCarthy, Steve Gordon September 18, 1990 TMS-113
Wraparounds: Buster and Babs spin a special wheel to determine the characters that star in each segment. "Devil Doggie": Elmyra Duff mistakes Dizzy Devil for a dog and takes him home with her.
4 4 "Test Stressed" Rich Arons Jim Reardon, Stephen Langford, Paul Dini, Sherri Stoner Bryon Vaughns, Rich Arons, Chris Reccardi September 19, 1990 TMS-122
Wraparounds: Buster introduces the start of each segment. "Never Too Late to Loon": Plucky asks Shirley the Loon to turn him into Albert Einstein to help pass a math test, only to fail the test because Einstein had poor grades as a youth.
5 5 "The Buster Bunny Bunch" Art Leonardi Gordon Bressack, Charles M. Howell IV, Len Jansen, Sherri Stoner Chris Otsuki, Alfred Gimeno, John Dorman, Barry Caldwell September 20, 1990 TMS-119
Wraparounds: In a spoof of The Mickey Mouse Club, various characters sing about Buster. "Buffed Bunny": Presuming that Babs is showing excitement over a billboard for a gym, Buster tries to win her over by conditioning his body.
6 6 "Her Wacky Highness" Ken Boyer Sherri Stoner Barry Caldwell, Byron Vaughns September 21, 1990 TMS-108
After being punished for misbehavior at school for her lack of self-control, Babs runs off to Wackyland. She becomes queen of the land, but soon finds herself annoyed by the inhabitants' lack of self-control, and has to be rescued by Buster, Plucky and Hamton.
7 7 "Hollywood Plucky" Art Vitello Sherri Stoner TBA September 22, 1990 TMS-125
Wanting to get his movie script produced, Plucky travels to Hollywood with Hamton, and both are lured into jobs at a swanky celebrity restaurant but are quickly fired.
8 8 "Journey to the Center of Acme Acres" Art Leonardi Wayne Kaatz John Ahern, Alberto DeMello, Art Leonardi, Chris Otsuki September 24, 1990 TMS-138
A massive earthquake leaves ruins to Acme Acres and sends the characters to the center of the Earth, where they find that gremlin's are causing the earthquakes because their large gold nugget has been stolen by Montana Max.
9 9 "It's Buster Bunny Time" Art Leonardi Dale Hale, Rowby Goren, Jim Reardon, Paul Dini, Don Dougherty, Tom Ruegger Chris Otsuki, Alberto DeMello, Barry Caldwell September 25, 1990 TMS-131
Wraparounds: A parody of Howdy Doody with Buster as Buffalo Bob and Plucky as Howdy Doody. "Bag That Bunny": Elmyra pays Calamity Coyote to help her catch Buster.
10 10 "Stuff That Goes Bump in the Night" Art Leonardi Pamela Hickey, Dennys McCoy, Richard Mueller, Pat Allee, Ben Hurst, Tom Ruegger Tony Benedict, Alberto DeMello, Chris Otsuki September 26, 1990 A-127
Wraparounds: Buster and Babs scare each other. "Home Wrecker": After finding steel bars driven down into his burrow, Buster finds that Montana Max is building a house right over him.
11 11 "Looking Out for the Little Guy" Art Vitello Nicholas Hollander, Jim Reardon, Pat Allee, Ben Hurst, Tom Ruegger Bruce Timm, Douglas McCarthy, Bill Snelgrove September 27, 1990 W-133
Wraparounds: Buster describes each episode as an example of the characters looking out for those smaller than they are. "Awful Orphan": Elmyra discovers Sneezer in a basket on her doorstep and cares for him as if he were a baby.
12 12 "Starting from Scratch" Ken Boyer Wayne Kaatz, Tom Ruegger Barry Caldwell, Bryon Vaughns, John Dorman September 28, 1990 W-132
This episode is a parody of Don Bluth's An American Tail, only it's done with fleas instead of mice. A family of fleas becomes separated when their younger child lands on Furrball.
13 13 "Hare Raising Night" Art Vitello Gordon Bressack, Charles M. Howell IV Douglas McCarthy, Bruce Timm, Bill Snelgrove October 1, 1990 W-117
Buster, Babs, Plucky and Hamton attempt to stop a mad scientist named Dr. Gene Splicer, who performs mutation experiments on animals.
14 14 "Furrball Follies" Art Vitello Tom Minton, Sherri Stoner, Eddie Fitzgerald Bruce Timm, Douglas McCarthy, Bill Snelgrove October 2, 1990 A-105
Each of these shorts is linked to the other chronologically. In all three, Furrball attempts to find a home, while being chased by two bully cats named Amby and Floyd (who make their first appearances in this episode).
15 15 "The Acme Acres Zone" Ken Boyer Michael Reaves, Wayne Kaatz, Sherri Stoner, Tom Minton Byron Vaughns, Barry Caldwell, John Dorman October 3, 1990 W-128
Wraparounds: A spoof of The Twilight Zone with Buster as Rod Serling. "A Walk on the Flip Side": Montana Max dreams that he has become a rabbit.
16 16 "Life in the 90's" Rich Arons Paul Dini, Bob Carrau, Jim Reardon, Sherri Stoner, Tom Ruegger Byron Vaughns, Jim Fletcher, Chris Reccardi, Glen Hill October 4, 1990 A-126
Wraparounds: Buster introduces each segment, describing them as examples of life in the 1990s. "Whining Out": Buster, Babs, Plucky and Hamton attend a fancy restaurant called Attitude, where they are treated poorly by the staff.
17 17 "Rock 'n' Roar" Art Vitello Sheryl Scarborough, Jim Reardon, Kayte Kuch Douglas McCarthy, Bruce Timm October 5, 1990 W-109
The characters are playing soccer when Buster's ball falls down into a hole. In it, Buster finds what he believes to be hundreds of soccer balls, one of which he believes is his.
18 18 "Prom-ise Her Anything" Ken Boyer Paul Dini, Bob Carrau TBA October 8, 1990 W-124
After being asked to the prom by Babs, Buster tries to learn how to dance by watching an old Bugs Bunny routine, but performs them poorly until Bugs comes in and assists him.
19 19 "Hare Today, Gone Tomorrow" Ken Boyer, Eddie Fitzgerald Wayne Kaatz, Tom Ruegger, Gordon Bressack, Charles M. Howell IV TBA October 9, 1990 KC-101
Buster Bunny is brought home by Elmyra, but tries to escape when he realizes how awful it is. Before leaving, Buster frees the other pets Elmyra has locked up, but he himself is recaptured.
20 20 "Cinemaniacs!" Art Vitello, Ken Boyer Sherri Stoner, Tom Minton, Wayne Kaatz, Jim Reardon TBA October 10, 1990 A-102
Wraparounds: Buster and Babs "theater hop" at a multiplex while being chased by usher Montana Max "SuperBabs": A spoof of the Superman film franchise with Babs in the role of Superman and Montana Max as "Wex Wuthor".
21 21 "You Asked for It" Art Vitello, Art Leonardi, Eddie Fitzgerald Sherri Stoner, Tom Minton, Wayne Kaatz, Eddie Fitzgerald TBA October 11, 1990 W-104
Wraparounds: Buster and Babs describe the episode as a "viewer request" day. Plucky invents a machine to pick who will star in each segment, but becomes frustrated when it fails to pick him.
22 22 "Gang Busters" Ken Boyer Wayne Kaatz, Jim Reardon TBA October 12, 1990 KC-112
Montana Max and his gang frame Buster for stealing a slushie machine, and Plucky Duck acts as Buster's lawyer to prove him innocent.
23 23 "Citizen Max" Art Vitello Gordon Bressack, Charles M. Howell IV, Paul Dini, Tom Ruegger TBA October 15, 1990 W-121
A Citizen Kane parody with Montana Max in the role of Charles Foster Kane. Hamton interviews the other characters, asking why Max said "Acme," but is unable to find an answer.
