During the winter, a greedy cat that appears to be on his death bed is welcomed into Jon's home. Garfield gets jealous of this intruder, and decides to get him to leave by pretending that Jon is fattening him up for a soup recipe.
14b 1b "The Impractical Joker" September 16, 1989 201

14c 1c "Fat and Furry" September 16, 1989 201
After Garfield buys a lottery ticket with the number 77777779, the crew win the lottery and become famous. They end up being featured on "Lifestyles of the Fat and Furry".
15a 2a "Rip Van Kitty" September 16, 1989 202
During a picnic, Garfield tries to nap, and plays cruel tricks on Odie and Nermal when they disturb his sleep. Jon accuses Garfield of sleeping too much, which prompts a dream sequence in which Garfield naps for twenty years and ends up in the future, where Earth is invaded by Garfield lookalikes from the planet "Zizzabottawattawattaboinkboink Three."
15b 2b "Grabbity" September 16, 1989 202
Roy plays a prank on Wade, who wonders what would happen if the Law of Gravity (or "Grabbity" as Roy pronounced it) was repealed.
15c 2c "The Big Catnap" September 16, 1989 202
Jon is less than excited to receive his once-a-year delivery of his favorite homemade cookies from his mother. He tells Herman Post, the mailman, that Garfield tricks him every year with elaborate pranks to steal the cookies, and desperately attempts to make this the first time that he will get to taste them.
16a 3a "The Great Getaway" September 23, 1989 203
Jon visits New York City on a weekend away from Garfield, unaware that Garfield has snuck along. Matters get worse when Garfield realizes that Jon's date, a seemingly innocent woman named Rosalyn, is a pickpocket.
16b 3b "Scrambled Eggs" September 23, 1989 203
Orson narrates a previous event about Sheldon and how he was mistaken for a mother turtle's child (who, like Sheldon, was not hatched yet and has his legs sticking out).
16c 3c "Hansel and Garfield" September 23, 1989 203
When Nermal comes over for a visit, Garfield is forced to entertain him, and narrates the story of Hansel and Gretel.
17a 4a "Sludge Monster" September 23, 1989 204
Jon, Garfield, and Odie are forced to stay in an old and abandoned hotel after the car breaks down in front of it.
17b 4b "Fortune Kooky" September 23, 1989 204
Wade becomes superstitious after reading his paper fortunes from Chinese fortune cookies. Roy, sensing an opportunity for a prank, concocts a fake fortune and then stages its events to scare Wade and steal his and Orson's picnic lunch.
17c 4c "Heatwave Holiday" September 23, 1989 204
During an especially hot summer day, Garfield decides to think in cold ways. This eventually develops into a trend throughout the city of decorating for Christmas in the middle of July.
18a 5a "One Good Fern Deserves Another" September 30, 1989 205
After Garfield and Odie destroy all of Jon's ferns, he heads to the local greenhouse to purchase a new one. The nearly-blind botanist inadvertently gives him a rare meat-eating fern, which endangers everyone in the house as Garfield tries to watch Attack of the Giant Pod People.
18b 5b "Goody-Go-Round" September 30, 1989 205
Bo needs a record player so he can listen to some music, and Orson sets out to find him one for being such a good friend, where a chain reaction is put in effect after the giver wants something in return, with some catastrophic results.
18c 5c "The Black Book" September 30, 1989 205
Jon is excited to go on a date with a beautiful woman. He becomes upset when he calls her up and hears a man's voice on the line (the man is actually his date's brother).
19a 6a "The Legend of the Lake" September 30, 1989 206
Garfield hosts a tour of the National Cat Museum, and narrates a story about a prehistoric feline who was determined to cross a giant lake to reach the fabled "lasagna trees" that grew on a faraway island.
19b 6b "Double Oh Orson" September 30, 1989 206
Orson reads a spy novel, and his imagination turns him into "Double-oh-Orson", a parody of the James Bond film Thunderball. Also featured are "Pinfeather" (Roy), "W" (Wade), "The Countless Lanolin" (Lanolin), and a frozen Bo.
19c 6c "Health Feud" September 30, 1989 206
Garfield takes revenge on a stereotypical TV health/fitness show host when his influence on Jon becomes a threat to Garfield's lifestyle.
20a 7a "Binky Gets Cancelled!" October 7, 1989 207
When Binky the Clown's show is cancelled because of low ratings, he decides to take a new job as a handyman.
20b 7b "Show Stoppers" October 7, 1989 207
The Farm animals put on a talent show, where Wade has stage fright. Orson gets it as well when his brothers attend the show.
20c 7c "Cutie and the Beast" October 7, 1989 207
Nermal runs away after believing that he is ugly, which is caused by one of Garfield's pranks. Odie is upset and nags Garfield into finding Nermal, which he eventually sets out to do.
21a 8a "The Lasagna Zone" October 7, 1989 208
Jon buys a new TV satellite dish, and when Garfield spills lasagna on its surface, he is transported into the TV shows.
21b 8b "Sleepytime Pig" October 7, 1989 208
Orson is tired during the day. Some of the others attempt to help him get rest, while the Sandman's substitute tries to make Orson go to sleep.
21c 8c "Yojumbo" October 7, 1989 208
Jon and Liz go on a date where they run into a bully, prompting Jon to feel like a wimp. He decides to take karate lessons, so he can stand up to the bully with his skills.
22a 9a "Pros and Cons" October 14, 1989 209
After being assigned to buy groceries, Odie is cheated out of his money by an alley cat, who trades an old sock in for Odie's grocery money.
22b 9b "Rooster Revenge" October 14, 1989 209
After pulling several pranks on Orson, Roy fears that Orson will pull a revenge prank on him, despite Orson's statement that he would not.
22c 9c "Lights! Camera! Garfield!" October 14, 1989 209
Garfield accepts a job as a movie stunt double after he thinks that he will be famous with larger roles.
23a 10a "Polecat Flats" October 14, 1989 210
Jon takes Garfield to a dude ranch called "Polecat Flats", where Garfield learns about the ranch life, while wondering what a polecat is (a skunk).
23b 10b "Hogcules" October 14, 1989 210
Orson tells Booker and Sheldon the myth of Hercules. Roy tells him his brothers are coming to visit and then Orson falls asleep, wondering what being Hercules would be like.
23c 10c "Brain Boy" October 14, 1989 210
Garfield is framed for destruction by Roscoe, a polite, clever and nerdy son of Jon's cousin Al, who turns out to be two-faced and ruthless.
24a 11a "Maine Course" October 21, 1989 211
A lady from an aquarium sends Jon a live lobster to cook. Jon, Garfield, and Odie eventually befriend it as a family member and name him Therm.
24b 11b "No Laughing Matter" October 21, 1989 211
After Roy annoys the farm animals with a new joke book, aliens who lack a sense of humor visit the farm to destroy comedy.
24c 11c "Attack of the Mutant Guppies" October 21, 1989 211
After hearing Garfield's story on giant radioactive mutant guppies, Nermal gets too scared to sleep. As an apology, Garfield tries to prove to Nermal that the story is fake.
25a 12a "Robodie" October 21, 1989 212
A mad scientist kidnaps Odie to use him as a model for his cloning machine that makes toy robots. Later, Garfield mistakes one for Odie.
25b 12b "First Aid Wade" October 21, 1989 212
After falling off the back of a cow, Wade injures his leg and thinks that Roy and Orson will perform surgery on it due to a misunderstanding.
25c 12c "Video Victim" October 21, 1989 212
Garfield and Jon make a bet that if Garfield can stop watching TV for 24 hours, he'll stop using the vacuum, in which annoys Garfield.
26a 13a "The Curse of Klopman" October 28, 1989 213
Jon's third cousin Norbert has died, and has left the Klopman Diamond in his will, which goes to Garfield. The will warns it has a curse on it.
26b 13b "Mud Sweet Mud" October 28, 1989 213
Everyone in the farm chooses to clean up the farm after it looks messy. When it comes to Orson's waller, Orson explains to them what it means to him.
26c 13c "Rainy Day Dreams" October 28, 1989 213
During a rainy night, Jon has a date at home, despite the fact that his date is a home insurance salesman.
27a 14a "Basket Brawl" October 28, 1989 214
Set as a basketball game that mice attend, Jon, Odie, and Nermal attempt to pack a picnic lunch without Garfield devouring the food.
27b 14b "Origin of Power Pig!" October 28, 1989 214
Orson tells Wade the story of how he originated Power Pig, while his brothers get an idea for stealing the harvest crops.
27c 14c "Cactus Jake Rides Again" October 28, 1989 214
Jon, Odie, and Garfield invite Cactus Jake to reside at their home temporarily during a rodeo championship, which they find unpleasant.

"Stinky" Davis, a common crook, decides to disguise himself as Binky the Clown to commit all kinds of crimes around town.
28b 15b "Barn of Fear" November 4, 1989 215

28c 15c "Mini-Mall Matters" November 4, 1989 215
Garfield hosts a factual and educational show about mini-malls and how they are grown, maintained, and their features.

29c 16c "It Must be True!" November 4, 1989 216
At the WBOR TV station, Garfield presents a show to a live audience called It Must be True!, where he claims everything stated on television is a true fact.

Garfield, Jon, and Odie visit Cactus Jake on his ranch. Cactus Jake shares a story about a cat he encountered, which was similar to Garfield.
32b 19b "Hogcules II" November 18, 1989 219
Roy tricks Wade into spilling grain from a silo, and Orson ends up trying to clean it up. He later daydreams about Hogcules dealing with a two-headed giant that stole a large birdbath.
32c 19c "Crime and Nourishment" November 18, 1989 219
When a picnic basket gets stolen, Garfield goes after it. He stumbles upon a strange community of small green people (similar to the Smurfs), who use food items as currency and eat money.
33a 20a "T.V. of Tomorrow" November 18, 1989 220
After their current set breaks, Jon, Garfield, and Odie go shopping for a new TV, some of which are bizarre. Guest voice of Frank Buxton.
33b 20b "Little Red Riding Egg" November 18, 1989 220
After finding a video camera, Orson plans to make a movie with the farm animals adapting Little Red Riding Hood using Sheldon as the title character, while the Fox is searching for nourishment.
33c 20c "Well Fed Feline" November 18, 1989 220
When an activist sees Jon outraged at Garfield for eating his lasagna cake (and hearing him say that he'll never feed Garfield again), she threatens to call the police unless Jon feeds Garfield as much as he can.
34a 21a "Invasion of the Big Robots" November 25, 1989 221
Garfield wakes up one morning and finds himself in a Space Shuttle Launch Site. After a warrior named Starwolf passes by him and makes commands to his fleet, Garfield soon realizes that he is in the wrong cartoon.
34b 21b "Shelf Esteem" November 25, 1989 221
After Wade crashes into his library, Orson plans to put all of his books away. Lanolin and Roy make a bet to see if Orson would finish the job that day.
34c 21c "Housebreak Hotel" November 25, 1989 221
Jon is going out of town and brings Garfield and Odie to Howie's Housebreak Hotel. Garfield is impressed with the hotel until he discovers that the animals are kept in cages and neglected.
35a 22a "First Class Feline" November 25, 1989 222
Jon and Odie see that Garfield has once again stuffed Nermal in a box marked to Abu Dhabi. The trio then teams up to trick Garfield into thinking he really has sent Nermal to the foreign country.
35b 22b "Hamelot" November 25, 1989 222
When the shower is broken, Orson decides to remedy this by getting an old bucket from the shed. Orson falls and loses consciousness when the pile that he is standing on collapses, leading him to dream of a Camelot-like setting.
35c 22c "How to be Funny!" November 25, 1989 222
Garfield gives a lecture on humor and what it means to be funny. He goes into detail on what is funny (silly costumes and gags such as Odie loving Mondays) and what is not funny (pie throwing and silly lighting jokes).
36a 23a "Mystic Manor" December 2, 1989 223
When Odie wanders into Mystic Manor, Garfield goes to get him back to avoid getting in trouble. Cameos of Orson, Wade, Roy, and Jim Davis.
36b 23b "Flop Goes the Weasel" December 2, 1989 223
A weasel is on the farm, lurking around to steal chickens. The farm animals believe Wade is a hero after inadvertently defeating the Weasel, causing Wade to take the fame for granted.
36c 23c "The Legend of Long Jon" December 2, 1989 223
While visiting Yorktown, Jon discovers that he apparently had a traitorous pirate ancestor named Long Jon Arbuckle. Guest voice of Shep Menken.
37a 24a "China Cat" December 2, 1989 224
While visiting China, a waitress at a restaurant tells Jon a legend about an orange cat similar to Garfield, and how it brings bad luck according to a dragon.
37b 24b "Cock-a-Doodle Dandy" December 2, 1989 224
Bo tells the viewers (and gets interrupted by characters who do not appear in the episode) about Roy, who is being attacked by a sleepy bear when trying to make his bugle wake-up call on time.
37c 24c "Beach Blanket Bonzo" December 2, 1989 224
Jon tries to impress a girl at the beach. Garfield does not understand how anyone could go crazy over a pretty face until he falls in love with a female cat named Lola.
38a 25a "Lemon Aid" December 16, 1989 225
Jon gets conned by Al Swindler into getting a new car, only to find that it is an old relic. Swindler then tries to sell Jon his old car back.
38b 25b "Hog Noon" December 16, 1989 225

38c 25c "Video Airlines" December 16, 1989 225
Garfield, Jon and Odie try to watch a good movie. The only movie on TV is "Kung Fu Creatures on the Rampage 2", which none of them are interested in.
39a 26a "The Mail Animal" December 16, 1989 226
After the latest prank by Garfield, Jon's mailman, Herman Post, gets fired by the post office chief for being a disgrace and afraid of a cat.
39b 26b "Peanut-Brained Rooster" December 16, 1989 226
Orson tells the story of how Roy became obsessed with peanuts. To make matters worse, Orson's brothers steal the peanut crop.
39c 26c "Mummy Dearest" December 16, 1989 226
While unconscious after a freak accident during a museum visit, Garfield dreams that he is a cat in ancient Egypt, where someone is trying get rid of him, as he is the good luck charm of the Pharaoh (Jon).
