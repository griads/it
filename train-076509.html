Reign Incumbent Notes
867 to 872 Ecgberht I puppet king of the Danes

876 to 877 Halfdan Ragnarsson Hálfdan in Old Norse, Halfdene or Healfdene in Old English, Albann in Old Irish. Norse ruler in York.
877 to 883 Interregnum
c. 883 to 895 Guthred Guðrøðr in Old Norse
late 9th century/early 10th century Siefredus Sigfroðr in Old Norse.
late 9th century/early 10th century Cnut Knútr in Old Norse. Sigfroðr and Knútr may have been joint kings for part or all of the period between 895 and 905
fl. c. 900—902 Æthelwold
c.902-910 Hálfdan and Eowils Another king, Ingwær, their brother, may have also ruled. All three were killed at the Battle of Tettenhall.
914 (or before) to 921 Ragnall Probably controlled much of Northumbria before finally becoming king of York in 918.
921 to 927 Sigtrygg Known as Sitric Cáech in Irish chronicles.
927 Gofraid ua Ímair Guthfrith in Old English. Guðrøðr in Old Norse.
927 to 939 Æthelstan of Wessex As King of the English
939 to 941 Olaf Guthfrithson son of Gofraid ua Ímair, known as Amlaíb mac Gofraid in Irish chronicles
941 to 943 or 944 Amlaíb Cuarán son of Sigtrygg, known in England as Olaf Sihtricson. Óláfr Sigtryggsson in Old Norse.
943 to 944 (with Amlaíb Cuarán?) Ragnall Guthfrithson Old Norse: Røgnvaldr Guðrøðsson; Old Irish: Ragnall mac Gofraid. Son of Gofraid ua Ímair
roughly 944 to 946 Eadmund of Wessex As King of the English
c. 947 to 948 Eric Bloodaxe
949 to 952 Amlaíb Cuarán restored

from 954 Eadred of Wessex Claiming and enforcing rule from 946; Kingdom absorbed permanently into England after 954
