List of The Muppet Show episodes


Section Title: Series 4 (1979–1980)
Table Section Text: None
No. Guest star Original air date
4.01 (73) John Denver 15 September 1979
Backstage: Kermit tries to convince the cast to go camping in the swamp. Song: "Why Can't We Be Friends?" Guest Act: "Garden Song" Swedish Chef: Squirrel Stew.
4.06 (74) Linda Lavin 22 September 1979
Backstage: Miss Piggy creates chaos among the cast as she co-opts the show as a surprise This is Your Life-style birthday tribute to Kermit.
4.03 (75) Shields and Yarnell 29 September 1979
Backstage: The mime guests alternately confuse and inspire the cast, especially Fozzie. Song: Bouncing birds-on-a-wire sing "Take a Chance on Me".
4.02 (76) Crystal Gayle 6 October 1979
Backstage: Scooter's Prairie Dog Glee Club run amok backstage, stealing anything that isn't nailed down. Song: Various animals perform a German-flavored "Swanee".
4.10 (77) Kenny Rogers 13 October 1979
Backstage: Scooter's uncle sells the mineral rights for Kenny's dressing room to Arabs. Also, Kermit tries to take a more "active" role in the show, with disastrous consequences.
4.07 (78) Dudley Moore 20 October 1979
Backstage: Dudley Moore's musical robot "M.A.M.M.A". causes mayhem and resentment among the cast.
4.08 (79) Arlo Guthrie 27 October 1979
Backstage: The casts prepares for a quiet "family" evening, and the Swedish Chef attempts to cook a "home meal". Guest Act: "Grocery Blues".
4.05 (80) Victor Borge 3 November 1979
Backstage: Bobby Benson is arrested, and Miss Piggy tries to babysit his Baby Band. Song: Pigs (led by Link) and chickens (led by Gonzo) sing "Macho Man" as rival gangs.
4.09 (81) Beverly Sills 10 November 1979
Backstage: The cast looks forward to Beverly's classy contributions to their show, especially Miss Piggy. Act: At Kermit's unwitting invitation, Statler and Waldorf give the audience a taste of old-style vaudeville with a burlesque performance of "Take Ten Terrific Girls" and a smattering of jokes.
4.14 (82) Liza Minnelli 17 November 1979
Backstage: Kermit plays a hard-boiled private eye who gets embroiled in a murder mystery on a movie set. As expected, Fozzie provides worse than no help at all as a Patrolbear.
4.11 (83) Lola Falana 24 November 1979
Backstage: Gonzo is quitting the show for a film career in Bombay. With Scooter's help, Kermit finds a replacement act.
4.12 (84) Phyllis George 1 December 1979
Backstage: Phyllis hosts the first annual Muppet Awards (the "Freds"), and the competition brings out the best and worst in some of the cast.
4.04 (85) Dyan Cannon 2 February 1980
Backstage: Miss Piggy's dog Foo-Foo disappears after everyone 'passes the bark' on dog-sitting him. She becomes increasingly frantic and wrathful in her search for him.
4.18 (86) Christopher Reeve 9 February 1980
Backstage: Miss Piggy is enamoured of this week's Superman guest star, and Kermit becomes a little jealous. Song: Kermit sings "Disco Frog".
4.19 (87) Lynda Carter 16 February 1980
Backstage: Inspired by their Wonder Woman guest star, Scooter and the cast take a correspondence course: "How to Be a Superhero".
4.17 (88) Cast of Star Wars (Mark Hamill, Peter Mayhew, Anthony Daniels and Kenny Baker) 23 February 1980
Backstage: Luke Skywalker, C-3PO and R2-D2 break the theater's walls (including the fourth) in search of a kidnapped Chewbacca. Luke's "cousin" Mark Hamill tries to join in on the act.
4.13 (89) Dizzy Gillespie 1 March 1980
Backstage: Statler calls in sick (of the show), and Waldorf's wife Astoria takes his place. Also, the show is audited for noise levels after complaints about Floyd and Zoot's back alley jam sessions.
4.15 (90) Anne Murray 8 March 1980
Backstage: Scooter ignores the theater's ban on skateboards, which really gets things rolling on and off stage. Song: Miss Piggy, Link and the other pigs sing "I Get Around" as a leather-clad gang of road hogs.
4.16 (91) Jonathan Winters 15 March 1980
Backstage: The show may be under a Gypsy curse. Song: Pigs sing "Tiger Rag" with Butch and Sundance.
4.22 (92) Andy Williams 22 March 1980
Backstage: Everyone has heard about Kermit's engagement to Miss Piggy, except Kermit. Also, the show gets even "cheesier" than usual.
4.21 (93) Doug Henning 3 May 1980
Backstage: Fozzie tries his hand at some magic, with 'hare-raising' results that keep things hopping on and off stage. Song: Miss Piggy sings "It's Magic" with magic accompaniment by Kermit.
4.23 (94) Carol Channing 10 May 1980
Backstage: Miss Piggy copes with buyer's remorse over an ill-fitting pair of new shoes. Song: Big-eyed Muppets help Carol sing a medley: "Jeepers Creepers" / "I, Yi, Yi, Yi, Yi (I Like You Very Much)" / "Them There Eyes".
4.24 (95) Diana Ross 17 May 1980
Backstage: Statler and Waldorf decide to keep score, and the audience is harder than usual to please. Song: Various birds and reptiles perform "I Go to Rio".
4.20 (96) Alan Arkin 24 May 1980
Backstage: Alan accidently drinks Dr Bunsen Honeydew's Jekyll-and-Hyde potion. Song: "The Devil Went Down to Georgia".
