Multiverse (Marvel Comics)


Section Title: Main alternate universes
Table Section Text: Below is a short, non-comprehensive list of the main universes in the Marvel Multiverse:
Name First Appearance Notes
Earth-616 Motion Picture Funnies Weekly #1 (1939) The setting of the Marvel Universe. Reality of the original Marvel Super Heroes and its mainstream continuity.
Earth-1610 Ultimate Spider-Man #1 (2000) The Ultimate Marvel universe is the reinvention of the Marvel Universe for the modern age. Initially beginning with Ultimate Spider-Man and Ultimate X-Men.
Earth-92131 Night of the Sentinels (Part 1) (October 31, 1992) Setting of X-Men: The Animated Series, Spider-Man: The Animated Series and (possibly) other animated shows of the early 1990s. First numbered in Volume 5 of the Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A-Z hardcover.
Earth-199999 Iron Man (May 2, 2008) The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is the continuity of The Avengers, Guardians of the Galaxy, Phil Coulson and the Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., Runaways, Spider-Man, Doctor Strange, Black Panther, Captain Marvel, Ant-Man and the Wasp, Cloak & Dagger, Agent Carter, and many other characters.
Earth-12041 Great Power and Great Responsibility (April 1, 2012) Universe of the animated series featured on Disney XD. Setting of Ultimate Spider-Man, Avengers Assemble, Hulk and the Agents of S.M.A.S.H., and Guardians of the Galaxy.
Earth-8311 Marvel Tails #1 (1983) The Larval Universe is the home reality of Peter Porker, the Spectacular Spider-Ham and intelligent, talking, anthropomorphic funny animal parodies of the Marvel characters.
Earth-928 Spider-Man 2099 #1 (1992) Alternate future of Earth-616 set in 2099, with futuristic incarnations of Marvel heroes, villains and teams. First numbered in Marvel Encyclopedia: Fantastic Four.
Earth-982 What If? vol. 2 #105 (1998) The setting of MC2. Home reality of Spider-Girl, J2, A-Next, Wild Thing, the Fantastic Five and other descendants of the Marvel heroes and villains.
Earth-2149 Ultimate Fantastic Four #21 (2005) Reality of the original Marvel Zombies series where an outbreak of a zombie virus turned all superheroes and supervillains into cannibalistic zombies.
Earth-712 Avengers #85–86 (February–March 1971) Home universe of the Squadron Supreme, a superhero team that is a pastiche of the Justice League and other DC Comics characters.
Earth-1287 Strikeforce: Morituri #01 (1986) Reality where a scientist named Dr. Kimmo Tuolema in 2072 discovers a process which can provide humans with superhuman powers in order to fight back a ravaging alien invasion that started in 2069.
Earth-88194 Dr. Zero #1 (1988) The setting of Shadowline. Created by Archie Goodwin under the Epic Comics imprint as a mature-themed line for Marvel Comics.
Earth-45828 Razorline: The First Cut #1 (Sept. 1993) Clive Barker's Razorline imprint, home reality of Ectokid, Saint Sinner, Hyperkind and Hokum & Hex. First numbered in Alternate Universes 2005.
Earth-807128 Wolverine vol. 3 #66 (Aug. 2008) The setting of Old Man Logan where the supervillains united and killed every superhero, then divided up the world into their respective territories.
Earth-90214 Spider-Man Noir #1 (Feb. 2009) The setting of Marvel Noir, a universe in the 1920s-1930s that has few superhumans who developed superpowers. This universe combines elements of film noir and pulp fiction.
Earth-65 Edge of Spider-Verse #2 (Nov. 2014) A reality in which Gwen Stacy was bitten by a radioactive spider and became Spider-Woman.
Earth-18119 Amazing Spider-Man: Renew Your Vows #3 (Oct. 2015) A reality that was briefly used for the Regency in Battleworld. In this reality, Spider-Man and Mary Jane had a daughter named Annie and most of the superheroes were defeated and captured by Regent.
Earth-1218 Marvel Team-Up #137 (Jan. 1984) The setting of our reality, in which superheroes and supervillains do not exist.
Earth-1048 Marvel's Spider-Man (September 2018) The setting of Spider-Man from Marvel's Spider-Man for PS4 in which Peter has been Spider-Man for eight years. There are also references to the Avengers, the Fantastic Four, the X-Men and the Inhumans.
Earth-79203 Battle Fever J (1979-1980) The setting of Toei's Super Sentai, partially inspired by Captain America.
