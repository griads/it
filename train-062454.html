Commissioners and Governors of the East Africa Protectorate
1 Sir Arthur Henry Hardinge (1859–1933) 1 Jul 1895 7 Oct 1900 British Foreign Office assumes control of Imperial British East Africa Company territory Construction begins of Uganda Railway Founding of Nairobi, Port Florence
– C.H. Craufurd 7 Oct 1900 30 Dec 1900 Acting Commissioner
2 Sir Charles Eliot (1861–1931) 30 Dec 1900 20 May 1904 Uganda transfers its Eastern Province to the East Africa Protectorate Founding of Kericho, Nakuru and Nyeri The African Standard established Native Civil hospital founded
– Frederick John Jackson (1860–1929) 20 May 1904 1 Aug 1904 Acting Commissioner
3 Sir Donald William Stewart (1860–1905) 1 Aug 1904 1 Oct 1905 First Maasai Treaty Administration transferred from Foreign Office to Colonial Office
– Frederick John Jackson (1860–1929) 1 Oct 1905 12 Dec 1905 Acting Commissioner
4 Sir James Hayes Sadler (1827–1910) 12 Dec 1905 12 Apr 1909 Government House built Office of Commissioner replaced with office of Governor Establishment of Legislative Council of Kenya Legislative Council publishes Report of the Land Commission Colonial office accepts principle that White Highlands be reserved for European settlement Capital moved from Mombasa to Nairobi
– Sir Charles Calvert Bowring (1872–1945) 12 Apr 1909 16 Sep 1909
5 Sir Percy Girouard (1867–1932) 16 Sep 1909 17 Jul 1912 Second Maasai Treaty
– Sir Charles Calvert Bowring (1872–1945) 17 Jul 1912 3 Oct 1912 Acting Governor

– Sir Charles Calvert Bowring (1872–1945) 14 Apr 1917 1 Feb 1919 Acting Governor

Governors of The Colony and Protectorate of Kenya
1 Sir Edward Northey (1868–1953) 11 Jun 1920 28 Aug 1922 East Africa Protectorate renamed The Colony and Protectorate of Kenya. 10 mile coastal strip under the domain of the Sultan of Zanzibar becomes a Protectorate.
2 Sir Robert Coryndon (1870–1925) 31 Aug 1922 10 Feb 1925 Devonshire White Paper Franchise extended to Indians and Arabs.
– Edward Brandis Denham (1876–1938) 10 Feb 1925 3 Oct 1925 Acting Governor Jubaland ceded to Italian Somaliland
3 Edward Grigg (1879–1955) 3 Oct 1925 27 Sep 1930 Native Lands Trust Ordinance Rudolf Province ceded from Uganda Coryndon Museum opened
– Sir Henry Monck-Mason Moore (1887–1964) 27 Sep 1930 13 Feb 1931 Acting Governor
4 Sir Joseph Byrne (1874–1942) 13 Feb 1931 22 Dec 1936
– Sir Armigel Wade (1880–1966) 22 Dec 1936 6 Apr 1937 Acting Governor
5 Sir Robert Brooke-Popham (1878–1953) 6 Apr 1937 9 Jan 1940
– Walter Harragin 30 Sep 1939 9 Jan 1940 Acting Governor
6 Sir Henry Monck-Mason Moore (1887–1964) 9 Jan 1940 25 Oct 1944 Founding of Kenya African Union
7 Sir Philip Mitchell (1890–1964) 11 Dec 1944 21 Jun 1952 East African High Commission established Nairobi National Park established
– Henry Steven Potter 21 Jun 1952 29 Sept 1952 Acting Governor
8 Sir Evelyn Baring (1903–1973) 30 Sept 1952 4 Oct 1959 Mau Mau Uprising begins The Lyttleton Constitution Franchise extended to Africans Nairobi Embakasi Airport opened
– Walter Coutts (1912–1988) 4 Oct 1959 23 Oct 1959 Acting Governor
9 Sir Patrick Muir Renison (1911–1965) 23 Oct 1959 17 Nov 1962 Mau Mau Uprising ends Founding of Kenya African National Union and Kenya African Democratic Union
– Sir Eric Griffith-Jones 17 Nov 1962 4 Jan 1963 Acting Governor
10 Malcolm MacDonald (1901–1981) 4 Jan 1963 12 Dec 1964 Kenya becomes a dominion MacDonald becomes Governor General of Kenya (only holder of post) briefly on 12 December 1964 and replaced the same day.
